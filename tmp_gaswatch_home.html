How We Track Prices

Our data comes directly from the DOE weekly price monitoring, made sharper by live updates from fellow Filipinos.

01

DOE Publishes

The Department of Energy releases weekly retail petroleum price advisories every Tuesday.

02

We Update

Our team updates the database with the latest prices from the DOE advisory within 24 hours.

03

Filipinos Report

Fellow drivers submit real pump prices from their area, keeping data accurate between DOE updates.

04

You Compare

View the map, compare prices by brand and area, and find the cheapest station near you.

About GasWatch PH

GasWatch PH is a free fuel price tracker built for Filipino drivers. We track gas stations across all 17 cities of Metro Manila, combining DOE official advisories with real prices reported by the community. No app needed — just open the site and find the cheapest fuel near you.

Frequently Asked Questions

Prices come from the DOE (Department of Energy) weekly price advisory released every Tuesday, and community-reported pump prices submitted by drivers on the site.
DOE official prices are updated every Tuesday. Community pump prices are updated in real time — anyone can submit the price they see at a station near them.
A community price is the price reported by someone who visited that station. These are often more accurate than official advisories because prices can vary per station.
DOE prices are official government advisories. Community prices are real pump prices reported by drivers. They reflect what you will actually pay at the station, which may differ from the DOE rate.
Tap any gas station on the map or in the price list, then tap "Update Price" on the station card. Fill in the price you paid and hit Submit. No account needed. For concerns, corrections, or anything else, use the Contact form at the bottom of the page. We read every message.
